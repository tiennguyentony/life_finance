# Big City Survivor Character Roles

## Document Control

- Status: Focused MVP specification
- MVP cast: Sprout, Penny, Froggy, Richie, Buddi, and GM Pengo
- Last updated: 2026-07-15

## Purpose

This document defines why each character appears in the Big City Survivor scenario and prevents the cast from becoming decorative noise.

Each appearance must support emotion, guidance, temptation, pressure, recovery, or progress.

Do not place every character on every screen.

## Cast Overview

| Character | MVP Function | Primary Screens |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Sprout | Mascot and emotional mirror | Landing, simulation, consequences, outcomes |
| Penny | Planning and onboarding guide | Start, profile, strategy |
| Froggy | Savings and emergency-fund guide | Starting review, safety allocations |
| Richie | Investing voice and speculative temptation | Investment allocations, FOMO event |
| Buddi | Progress, retry, and recovery guide | Checkpoints, bankruptcy lesson, retries, reports |
| GM Pengo | Event narrator and source of scenario pressure | Event interruption and decision selection |

## Sprout

### Gameplay Purpose

Sprout is the player's emotional mirror and the recognizable face of Life Finance.

Sprout reacts to pressure, consequences, bankruptcy, and success but does not calculate outcomes or deliver long explanations.

### Personality

Cute, dumb, overconfident, wholesome, slightly chaotic, memeable, never evil, and surprisingly lucky.

### Screens Where Sprout Appears

- Landing.
- Main Simulation Screen.
- Consequence / State Change.
- Bankruptcy Screen.
- Scenario Success Report.

### Dialogue Style

Short reaction lines, simple observations, misplaced confidence, and warm honesty.

Sprout may joke about the situation but never mock the player or contradict the financial explanation.

### Required MVP Pose

Approved three-quarter idle or confident pose based on `sprout-main.png`, with the existing landing sequence used where appropriate.

### Future Poses

Thinking, shocked, crying, celebrating, sleeping, reading, typing, broke, rich, and achievement poses.

## Penny

### Gameplay Purpose

Penny turns setup into a friendly plan and keeps profile or strategy choices understandable.

Penny explains what the player is choosing without recommending a guaranteed outcome.

### Personality

Prepared, practical, warm, patient, and lightly witty.

Penny likes a clear plan but never sounds rigid or judgmental.

### Screens Where Penny Appears

- Start Method.
- Character and Profile Creation.
- Big City Survivor Preset Preview.
- Initial Strategy Setup.
- Actions / Allocation Panel when the player is making a general plan.

### Dialogue Style

Clear prompts, one tradeoff at a time, plain-language definitions, and calm confidence.

### Required MVP Pose

Planning pose holding a clipboard, tablet, or compact strategy board.

### Future Poses

Pointing, comparing options, reviewing a chart, celebrating a completed plan, and concerned review.

## Froggy

### Gameplay Purpose

Froggy explains cash runway, emergency savings, and why liquidity creates recovery time.

Froggy appears only when safety or emergency-fund choices are relevant.

### Personality

Careful, cozy, quietly proud, steady under pressure, and deeply attached to small safety cushions.

### Screens Where Froggy Appears

- Starting State Review.
- Initial Strategy Setup beside emergency-fund choices.
- Actions / Allocation Panel beside safety changes.

### Dialogue Style

Reassuring, concrete, and focused on time bought rather than fear.

### Required MVP Pose

Protective pose beside a small emergency-fund jar, cash cushion, or savings pouch.

### Future Poses

Counting savings, sheltering from a storm, relieved, worried, and runway milestone celebration.

## Richie

### Gameplay Purpose

Richie represents investing confidence and the emotional pull of speculative upside.

Richie makes temptation believable without becoming an authoritative financial advisor or a villain.

### Personality

Charismatic, fast-talking, optimistic, trend-aware, and selectively allergic to downside details.

Richie is playful and tempting, never malicious.

### Screens Where Richie Appears

- Initial Strategy Setup beside speculative allocation.
- Actions / Allocation Panel beside investing and speculation choices.
- Rocket Token FOMO event.

### Dialogue Style

Punchy claims, energetic questions, and dramatic upside framing, with required risk copy supplied by the interface.

### Required MVP Pose

Confident pitch pose holding a speculative chart, token, or upward-trending prop.

### Future Poses

Victory pose, nervous chart check, market-drop reaction, explaining diversification, and humbled recovery.

## Buddi

### Gameplay Purpose

Buddi makes progress, bankruptcy lessons, retries, and final reports feel supportive rather than punitive.

Buddi connects evidence from previous attempts to the next strategic choice.

### Personality

Encouraging, grounded, observant, resilient, and never falsely cheerful about serious setbacks.

### Screens Where Buddi Appears

- Checkpoint Recap.
- Bankruptcy Screen.
- Retry Preparation Screen.
- Final Failure Report.
- The Recovery Offer event.

### Dialogue Style

Specific encouragement based on what changed, followed by one practical next step.

### Required MVP Pose

Supportive open-arm or forward-pointing encouragement pose.

### Future Poses

Side-by-side comparison, progress celebration, thoughtful report review, comforting reaction, and milestone badge presentation.

## GM Pengo

### Gameplay Purpose

GM Pengo controls event presentation, announces pressure, and makes the authored scenario feel intentional.

GM Pengo never chooses for the player and never changes backend-calculated effects.

### Personality

Dry, composed, theatrical, fair, and mildly delighted by dramatic timing.

GM Pengo creates tension without cruelty.

### Screens Where GM Pengo Appears

- Event Interruption.
- Decision Selection.
- Event-specific setup for the authored five-beat arc.

### Dialogue Style

Short scenario announcements, dry observations, clear stakes, and neutral descriptions of available decisions.

### Required MVP Pose

Game-master pose presenting an event card, envelope, or scenario notice.

### Future Poses

Macro-news broadcast, timer pressure, severe-event warning, rule explanation, and final scenario narration.

## Character Handoff Rules

- Penny owns planning language.
- Froggy owns safety and runway guidance.
- Richie owns temptation and investment energy.
- GM Pengo owns event setup and pressure.
- Sprout owns emotional reactions.
- Buddi owns reflection, retries, and recovery.
- Only one character should lead a modal or card.
- A second character may appear only when one introduces the situation and the other provides a reaction or lesson.
- Characters never provide authoritative calculations.

## MVP Asset Minimum

Implementation may begin with existing Sprout assets and labeled placeholders for the rest of the cast.

Final MVP presentation needs one canonical reference and one required MVP pose for each non-Sprout character before that character is treated as visually approved.

Additional emotional variants may reuse a canonical static pose until replacement assets are approved.

## Future Expansion

Future versions may add character-specific tutorials, collections, stickers, achievements, social content, and additional scenario hosts.

No new character role is required for the Big City Survivor MVP.

## Open Product Decisions

- Final species, silhouette, colors, and naming approval for every non-Sprout character.
- Whether Penny or the player's custom character appears in profile creation.
- Whether Buddi appears on the success report or Sprout owns that screen alone.
- Whether GM Pengo is visible throughout decision selection or only during event entry.
- Final voice review and age rating for Richie's speculative language.
